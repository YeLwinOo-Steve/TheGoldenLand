WELCOME TO

THE GOLDEN LAND

MYANMAR, also known as The Golden Land, is situated in South-East Asia. It is rich in natural resources and beautiful nature. Myanmar citizens are mostly Buddhists and are not only very generous but also hospitable.

Read More

RESOURCES

Myanmar has enormous amount of natural resources including ranges of mountains, beautiful forests, rivers and breathtaking views.

CULTURE

A long and rich cultural life runs through Myanmar's long and rich history, with Buddhist architecture the most dazzling example of its creativity.

RELIGION

Buddhism in Myanmar is predominantly of the Theravada tradition, practised by 88% of the country's population. It is the most religious Buddhist country.

LANGUAGE

There are about a hundred languages in Myanmar. Burmese is the official language. It is spoken by two thirds of Myanmar people.

ETHNIC GROUPS

States

7 STATES

Kachin State

Kachin State is the northernmost state of Myanmar.
It is bordered by China to the north and east; Shan State to the south; and Sagaing Region and India to the west.


Area - 89,041 km2 (34,379 sq mi)
Capital - Myitkyina
Places
- Hkakabo Razi Mountain(the highest mountain in Myanmar)
- Indawgyi Lake

Kayah State

Kayah State is situated in eastern Myanmar.

It is bounded on the north by Shan State, on the east by Thailand's Mae Hong Son Province, and on the south and west by Kayin State.


Area - 11,670 km2 (4,510 sq mi)
Capital - Loikaw
Places
- Taung Kwe Zayde
- Thiri Mingalarpon Kyaung

Kayin State

Kayin State is in the southern Myanmar.It is bordered by Mae Hong Son, Tak, and Kanchanaburi provinces of Thailand to the east; Mon State and Bago Region to the west and south; Mandalay Region, Shan State and Kayah State to the north


Area - 30,383 km2 (11730.9 sq mi)
Capital - Hpa-An
Places
- Mount Zwegabin
- Bayint Nyi Cave

Chin State

Chin State is a state in western Myanmar.
It is bordered by Sagaing Division and Magway Division to the east, Rakhine State to the south, Bangladesh to the south-west, and the Indian states of Mizoram to the west and Manipur to the north.


Area - 36,019 km2 (13,907 sq mi)
Capital - Hakha
Places
- Nat Ma Taung (Mt. Victoria)
- Rieh (Rih) Lake

Mon State

Mon State is in the southern part of Myanmar.
It lies between Kayin State to the east, the Andaman Sea to the west, Bago Region to the north and Tanintharyi Region to the south, also having a short border with Thailand.


Area - 12,155 km2 (4693 sq mi)
Capital - Mawlamyine
Places
- Kyaiktiyo Pagoda
- Setse Beach

Rakhine State

Rakhine State is situated on the western coast.
It is bordered by Chin State to the north, Magway Region, Bago Region and Ayeyarwady Region to the east, the Bay of Bengal to the west and the Chittagong Division of Bangladesh to the northwest.


Area - 36,762 km2 (14,194 sq mi)
Capital - Sittwe
Places
- Ngapali Beach
- Marauk-U

Shan State

Shan State is the easternmost state of Myanmar.

It borders China to the north, Laos to the east, and Thailand to the south, and five administrative divisions of Burma in the west.


Area - 155,800 km2 (60154.7 sq mi)
Capital - Taunggyi
Places
- Inle Lake
- Faungdawoo Pagoda

Regions

7 REGIONS

Yangon Region

Yangon Region is located in the heart of Lower Myanmar.

The division is bordered by Bago Region to the north and east, the Gulf of Martaban to the south, and Ayeyarwady Region to the west.


Area - 10,170 km2 (3,930 sq mi)
Capital - Yangon
Places
- Shwedagon Pagoda
- Kandawgyi Lake

Mandalay Region

Mandalay Region is located in the center of the country.

It is bordered by Sagaing Region and Magway Region to the west, Shan State to the east, and Bago Region and Kayin State to the south.


Area - 37,946 km2 (14651 sq mi)
Capital - Mandalay
Places
- Mahamuni Buddha Temple
- Mandalay Hill

Sagaing Region

Sagaing Region is located in the north-western part of Myanmar.
It is bordered by India to the north, Kachin State, Shan State, and Mandalay Region to the east, Mandalay Region and Magway Region to the south, and Chin State and India to the west.


Area - 93,527 km2 (36111 sq mi)
Capital - Sagaing
Places
- Kaunghmudaw Pagoda
- Mingun Bell

Magway Region

Magway Region is located in the central Myanmar.
It is bordered by Sagaing Region is to its north, Mandalay Region to its east, Bago Region to its south and Rakhine State and Chin State to its west.


Area - 44,820 km2 (17,306 sq mi)
Capital - Magway
Places
- Minbu-Shwesettaw
- Magway Myathalun

Tanintharyi Region

Tanintharyi Region covers the long narrow southern part of Myanmar.

It borders the Andaman Sea to the west and the Tenasserim Hills, beyond which lie Thailand, to the east. To the north is the Mon State.


Area - 43,344.9 km2 (16,735.6 sq mi)
Capital - Dawei
Places
- Nyaung Oo Phee Island
- Horse Shoe Island

Ayeyarwady Region

Ayeyarwady Region occupies the delta region of the Ayeyarwady River (Irrawaddy River).
It is bordered by Bago Region to the north, Bago Region and Yangon Region to the east and the Bay of Bengal to the south and west. It is contiguous with the Rakhine State in the northwest.


Area - 35,140 km2 (13,566 sq mi)
Capital - Pathein
Places
- Chaung Thar Beach
- Ngwe Saung Beach

Bago Region

Bago Region is located in the southern central part of the country.
It is bordered by Magway Region and Mandalay Region to the north; Kayin State, Mon State and the Gulf of Martaban to the east; Yangon Region to the south and Ayeyarwady Region and Rakhine State to the west.


Area - 39,402.3 km2 (15,213.3 sq mi)
Capital - Bago
Places
- Shawmawdaw Pagoda
- Shwethalyaung Buddha

Rivers

4 MAJOR RIVERS

Ayeyarwady River

Ayeyarwady River is a river that flows from north to south through Myanmar. It is the country's largest river originating from the confluence of the N'mai and Mali rivers.


Length - 2,210 km (1,370 mi)

Chindwin River

Chindwin River is a river in Burma (Myanmar), and the largest tributary of the country's chief river the Ayeyarwady (Irrawaddy). It flows entirely within Burma and is known as Ning-thi to the Meiteis.


Length - 1,207 km (750 mi)

Salween River ( Thanlwin River)

Salween River ( Thanlwin River), known in China as the Nu River, is a river about 2,815 kilometres (1,749 mi) long that flows from the Tibetan Plateau into the Andaman Sea in Southeast Asia.


Length - 2,815 km (1,749 mi)

Sittaung River

Sittaung River is a river in south central Myanmar in Bago Division. The river originates at the edge of the Shan Hills southeast of Mandalay, and flows southward to the Gulf of Martaban.


Length - 420 km (260 mi)
